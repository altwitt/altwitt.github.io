# How Low can you Go!
## The journey from the popular interpreted Python down to Assembly, the closest one can get to the machine!

![Assembly](../images/Assembly.png)
I have hit a point in my development career where I am feeling much more comfortable with higher-level languages (Python, JS). Because of this, I have committed myself in a hardcore way to learning low-level Assembly and C. I am feeling extremely positive about it.After watching some videos of Devon Crawford on youtube, I realized that this college student seems to have such a handle on a very wide range of projects. He coded an LED Music Visualizer and a battery-tester robot with Arduino and Java.  He also coded this badass timelapse solution for Photoshop. While I watched him, I felt great respect and joy for the guy. He has his whole life ahead of him. He is working on many things that he really seems to enjoy, and he carries himself very well and his videos are cool as shit. I mean the guy has 150,000 subscribers on youtube. Seems like that would be difficult no matter who you were, and there would be a point where your deeply ingrained personality traits would shin through either resulting in a lift up to great success or a drag down into disenchantment. Devon totally seems like the former. Anywho, I brought up Devon’s videos, because he made me realize that it wouldn’t be such a big jump from geoprocessing the robotics programming. My eyes have really been opened in a big way to other avenues of coding and computer science.
